Three Major Threat Categories

Modern cyberattacks fall into three primary categories. Click any card to learn more about each threat type.

🦠
Malware
Malicious software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to systems. Includes viruses, ransomware, trojans & spyware.
🎣
Phishing

// THREAT 01

🦠 Malware

Malware (malicious software) is any program intentionally designed to cause damage to a computer, server, or network.

Types of Malware

Each malware type has a different method of attack and level of damage.

🔴 Virus

Attaches to legitimate files and spreads when the file is executed. Corrupts data and slows systems.

🟠 Worm

Self-replicates across networks without human action. Consumes bandwidth and crashes servers.

🔴 Ransomware

Encrypts victim's files and demands payment (Bitcoin) for the decryption key.

🟣 Trojan

Disguises itself as legitimate software. Creates backdoors for attackers once installed.

🟠 Spyware

Silently monitors user activity, keystrokes, and steals passwords and personal data.

🔴 Rootkit

Hides deep in OS, gives attacker persistent admin-level access while evading detection.

Real-World Malware Attacks

Famous attacks that caused billions of dollars in damage worldwide.

MAY 2017

WannaCry Ransomware

Infected 230,000+ computers in 150 countries. Hit NHS UK, FedEx, Telefonica. Demanded Bitcoin ransom. Damage: $4–8 Billion.

JUNE 2017

NotPetya Wiper

Disguised as ransomware but was a wiper. Hit Maersk, Merck, FedEx. Caused $10 Billion+ in damage. Spread via MeDoc software update.

2020

SolarWinds Supply Chain

Trojan hidden inside SolarWinds Orion software update. Compromised 18,000 orgs including US Treasury, Pentagon & Microsoft.

2021

Colonial Pipeline Ransomware

DarkSide gang shut down largest US fuel pipeline. Caused fuel shortages across US East Coast. $4.4M ransom paid in Bitcoin.

🛡️ Malware Prevention Measures

  • 🔄
    Keep Software Updated
    Patch OS and apps regularly. WannaCry exploited an unpatched Windows flaw (MS17-010).
  • 🛡️
    Use Antivirus / EDR
    Tools like Windows Defender, CrowdStrike detect and quarantine malware in real time.
  • 💾
    Regular Backups (3-2-1 Rule)
    3 copies, 2 media types, 1 offsite. Defeats ransomware by restoring without paying.
  • 🚫
    Principle of Least Privilege
    Users should only have permissions they need. Limits malware's ability to spread.
  • 🔐
    Application Whitelisting
    Only allow pre-approved programs to run. Blocks unknown malware from executing.
  • 📧
    Email Filtering
    Block malicious attachments (.exe, .bat, macros). Most malware arrives via email.
// THREAT 02

🎣 Phishing

Phishing is a social engineering attack where attackers impersonate trusted entities to steal sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or login credentials.

Types of Phishing

Phishing comes in many forms, from broad email blasts to hyper-targeted attacks.

📧 Email Phishing

Mass emails impersonating banks, PayPal, or tech companies with fake login pages.

🎯 Spear Phishing

Targeted attack using victim's personal info to appear more legitimate and trustworthy.

🐋 Whaling

Targets high-profile executives (CEO, CFO). Often requests urgent wire transfers.

📱 Smishing

Phishing via SMS messages with fake delivery or bank alerts containing malicious links.

📞 Vishing

Voice phishing — attackers call victims pretending to be the IRS, bank, or tech support.

🌀 Pharming

Redirects users from legitimate websites to fake ones by poisoning DNS records.

Real-World Phishing Attacks

These attacks cost organizations billions and exposed millions of accounts.

2013–2015

Google & Facebook Scam ($100M)

Lithuanian hacker Evaldas Rimasauskas posed as Quanta Computer (real supplier). Sent fake invoices via email and stole $100M from both companies.

MARCH 2016

DNC Email Hack (US Election)

Russian hackers sent spear-phishing emails to John Podesta. Fake Google security alert captured his Gmail credentials, exposing 50,000 emails.

2020

Twitter Celebrity Bitcoin Hack

Attackers spear-phished Twitter employees via phone. Hijacked accounts of Obama, Musk, Gates. Stole $120,000 worth of Bitcoin in hours.

2022

Uber Data Breach

Attacker texted an Uber contractor pretending to be IT support. Got VPN credentials, accessed internal systems exposing 77,000 employee records.

🛡️ Phishing Prevention Measures

  • 🔑
    Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
    Even if password is stolen, MFA blocks login. Use authenticator apps, not SMS.
  • 🔗
    Verify URLs Before Clicking
    Hover over links to see real destination. Check for HTTPS and correct domain spelling.
  • 📚
    Security Awareness Training
    Regular phishing simulation drills for employees. Teach red flags: urgency, requests for credentials.
  • 📧
    Email Authentication (SPF/DKIM/DMARC)
    Prevents attackers from spoofing your domain. Marks or rejects fraudulent emails.
  • 🌐
    Browser Security Extensions
    Tools like Netcraft or Google Safe Browsing warn before opening known phishing sites.
  • 🚨
    Zero-Trust Policy for Wire Transfers
    Always verify financial requests via a secondary channel (phone call) — never email only.

💥 DDoS Attacks

A Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack floods a server or network with massive traffic from thousands of compromised machines (botnets), making services unavailable.

Types of DDoS Attacks

DDoS attacks target different layers of the network stack.

📦 Volumetric

Floods bandwidth using UDP/ICMP packets. Measured in Gbps. Ex: DNS Amplification, NTP flood.

⚡ Protocol Attack

Exploits weaknesses in Layer 3/4 protocols. Ex: SYN Flood, Ping of Death, Smurf Attack.

🌐 Application Layer

Targets Layer 7 (HTTP). Mimics real traffic. Ex: HTTP Flood, Slowloris, RUDY.

🤖 Botnet-based

Thousands of infected IoT devices (like Mirai's cameras/routers) used as attack sources.

🔊 Amplification

Attacker spoofs victim's IP and sends small queries to open servers that reply with huge responses.

🐌 Slowloris

Keeps many HTTP connections open using partial requests until the server runs out of connections.

Real-World DDoS Attacks

Some of the largest and most impactful DDoS attacks in history.

OCTOBER 2016

Dyn DNS — Mirai Botnet

600,000+ IoT devices (cameras, DVRs) used to take down Dyn DNS. Twitter, Netflix, Reddit, Spotify went offline for hours across US East Coast.

FEBRUARY 2018

GitHub — 1.35 Tbps Attack

Largest DDoS at the time using Memcached amplification. GitHub was offline for 10 minutes before Akamai's DDoS scrubbing filtered the traffic.

FEBRUARY 2020

AWS Shield — 2.3 Tbps

Largest DDoS attack ever recorded at time. AWS Shield mitigated the attack on an unnamed customer. Used CLDAP reflection technique.

MARCH 2023

CloudFlare — 71M rps

Record-breaking HTTP/2 DDoS hitting 71 million requests/second from 30,000+ IPs. Targeted gaming, crypto, and hosting providers globally.

🛡️ DDoS Prevention Measures

  • ⚡
    Rate Limiting
    Limit requests per IP per second. Nginx: limit_req_zone blocks flood traffic at the edge.
  • ☁️
    CDN / WAF (Cloudflare, Akamai)
    Absorbs and filters attack traffic globally before it reaches your server. Essential for any public service.
  • 🍪
    SYN Cookies
    Validates TCP handshakes without allocating memory. Defeats SYN flood attacks at the OS level.
  • 🔥
    Firewall / IP Blacklisting
    iptables or cloud security groups can block suspicious IP ranges. Auto-ban IPs hitting thresholds.
  • 📡
    Anycast Network Diffusion
    Routes DDoS traffic across multiple data centers, diluting the attack across the global network.
  • 📊
    Anomaly Detection
    Monitor traffic baselines. Alert when requests spike abnormally (like in your ddos-tool project!).

📅 Notable Cyberattack Timeline

A chronological look at the most impactful cyberattacks that shaped modern cybersecurity practices.

2013
Target Data Breach — 40 Million Cards Stolen
Malware installed on POS terminals via a phishing email sent to an HVAC contractor with Target network access.
2014
Sony Pictures Hack — North Korea
Destructive malware wiped Sony's systems, leaked unreleased films and personal employee data. Politically motivated.
2016
DNC Spear Phishing — US Election Interference
Russian APT used targeted phishing emails to compromise Democratic National Committee email accounts.
2016
Dyn DDoS — Internet Black Out (Mirai Botnet)
Mirai botnet of 600K+ IoT devices brought down Dyn DNS, crashing Twitter, Netflix & Reddit for millions of users.
2017
WannaCry Ransomware — 150 Countries Hit
Exploited EternalBlue (NSA exploit). Demanded Bitcoin. NHS UK had to cancel 19,000+ appointments. $4–8B damage.
2020
SolarWinds — Supply Chain Trojan
Trojan in a software update compromised 18,000 organizations including US government agencies for 9+ months undetected.
2021
Colonial Pipeline — Ransomware Fuel Crisis
DarkSide ransomware shut down 5,500 miles of fuel pipeline. US declared state of emergency. $4.4M ransom paid.
2023
Cloudflare — 71M req/sec DDoS Record
HTTP/2 DDoS attack hitting 71 million requests per second from 30,000+ source IPs — the largest ever recorded at the time.
